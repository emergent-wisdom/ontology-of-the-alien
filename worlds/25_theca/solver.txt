# The Drift-Tide Retirement System

## The Core Problem, Thecally Understood

In Theca, irregular earners face a compounded crisis. Their income arrives in unpredictable amounts, yes—but worse, their thin containment (poor housing, few nested walls) means they are already *becoming less real*. A retirement system must solve two problems simultaneously: accumulating value despite inconsistency, and preventing the worker from fading before they reach retirement at all.

Traditional thecal banking fails them. It requires extracting savings through multiple boundary layers with each deposit—each extraction a naked interval risk. For someone depositing sporadically, the accumulated risk of all those naked intervals often exceeds the value gained.

## The Solution: Collective Outer Thecae with Permeable Inner Membranes

### 1. The Guild-Shell Structure

Workers join a **Retirement Guild**—not an organization but literally a shared outer theca. The Guild maintains massive nested walls (typically five to seven layers) around a collective space. Your *membership itself* is a theca: a small ceramic token, inscribed with your identity, kept within the Guild's innermost chamber.

This token's location matters profoundly. While it rests within those seven walls, *you* gain ontological weight by extension—the Law of Boundary Pressure operates through sympathetic containment. A day laborer whose token sits in the Guild-Shell walks more solidly, speaks more audibly, exists more firmly than they would alone.

**The key innovation**: You never extract your token. It stays nested. Only contributions flow in and out.

### 2. Variable-Membrane Contribution Thecae

Each member receives a **Tide-Vessel**: a specially crafted theca made from woven sea-silk and flexible ceramic compounds. Unlike rigid containers that reject incompatible contents, Tide-Vessels are designed with *breathing boundaries*—they expand or contract based on what's deposited.

Critically, these vessels accept contributions of **any size** without the contents passing through as "incompatible." A single copper dropped in during a lean month doesn't rattle around, rejected and fading. The membrane *contracts* around it, holding it as firmly as it would hold a hundred gold pieces during a fortunate month.

The Tide-Vessel sits within the Guild-Shell's nested walls. Your erratic deposits gain seven layers of containment depth immediately upon entry—not through your own walls, but through the collective's.

### 3. The Tidal Protocol

Instead of demanding regular contributions (impossible for irregular earners), the system uses **Tidal Windows**: scheduled periods when the Guild-Shell's inner membranes become temporarily permeable.

During a Tidal Window:
- The outermost walls remain sealed (maintaining protection)
- Inner membranes soften, allowing deposits to flow inward without repeated naked intervals
- A Thecawright guides all contributions simultaneously through the layers
- Workers deposit whatever they have—much, little, or nothing

The genius: **the naked interval is shared**. When a hundred workers' contributions pass through each membrane together, the risk distributes. A single coin, crossing alone, might fade. A hundred coins crossing together anchor each other's reality through momentary mutual containment.

Those who deposit nothing during a Tidal Window lose nothing—their previous deposits remain nested, gaining ontological weight through continued depth. There is no penalty for inconsistency, only reward for contribution.

### 4. Drift-Clustering

Small contributions from multiple workers are combined into **Drift-Clusters**: shared inner thecae containing pooled deposits. Your three coppers join with another worker's five and another's two into a single contained unit.

This isn't communal ownership—Boundarists mark each contribution with identity-compatible tracers (typically hair or nail clippings sealed in wax, stored alongside the coins). But the cluster *exists more strongly* than individual tiny deposits would. Ten people's small contributions, held in one well-made inner theca, have more reality than ten separate fading fragments.

When retirement comes, the tracers guide the separation, and your share emerges proportional to what you contributed.

### 5. Depth-Dividends: Time as Container

Here is the profound leverage of Thecal physics: **time itself adds containment layers**.

A coin deposited into the Guild-Shell and left untouched for thirty years has been contained within those seven walls for thirty years. The Law of Boundary Pressure doesn't just count *layers*—it counts *duration of containment*. The coin hasn't earned interest. It has become *more real*.

A copper deposited in youth and left nested for decades emerges at retirement with the ontological weight of gold. This isn't alchemy—the metal hasn't changed. But its *existence-density* has increased. It purchases more because it *is* more.

This perfectly serves irregular earners. They needn't contribute consistently—they need only contribute *early*. A burst of deposits in a good year, left nested for three decades, outweighs steady contributions made recently.

### 6. The Unbinding Ceremony

At retirement, a Master Thecawright performs the **Unbinding**: carefully extracting your accumulated deposits through each layer of the Guild-Shell.

This is sacred work. The naked interval at each boundary must be precisely controlled—too fast, and contents scatter; too slow, and they begin to fade. The Thecawright extends or shortens each crossing based on the ontological weight of what passes through.

Heavily nested, time-deepened deposits survive the Unbinding easily. They have become so real that brief exposure barely dims them. This is why the system works for irregular earners: they may have contributed erratically, but the depth-dividends have made their retirement *sturdy enough to survive extraction*.

The unbound retirement fund is then placed in a **Personal Sunset Theca**: a final container, sized to the retiree's needs, designed to release small portions at regular intervals (each release a controlled micro-naked-interval, performed by local Thecawrights at Guild offices).

## Why This Works for Irregular Earners

1. **No extraction risk during accumulation**: Deposits flow in through Tidal Windows but never out until retirement. The naked intervals are collective, shared, and rare.

2. **Size-agnostic acceptance**: Tide-Vessels don't reject small or irregular contributions. Whatever you have becomes contained.

3. **Collective outer depth**: The Guild-Shell provides the nested walls that poor workers cannot afford individually. Your savings gain the ontological weight of a nobleman's vault.

4. **Time rewards patience, not consistency**: Depth-dividends favor early deposits over regular ones. A laborer who deposits heavily in their twenties, then sporadically for decades, retires wealthier than one who deposits steadily but starts late.

5. **Drift-Clustering prevents fading**: Small contributions don't wither alone—they're pooled with others, gaining mutual containment.

6. **Sympathetic containment preserves the person**: While your token rests in the Guild-Shell, *you* exist more firmly. The system doesn't just save money—it saves the worker from translucence, keeping them real enough to reach retirement.

---

The Drift-Tide system recognizes what traditional thecal banking ignores: that for irregular earners, the problem was never inconsistency of contribution. It was inconsistency of *containment*. By providing collective outer boundaries and time-rewarding depth, the system transforms erratic small deposits into ontologically dense retirement wealth—and keeps the depositors solid enough to claim it.
