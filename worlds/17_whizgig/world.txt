# The World of Whizgig

## The Core Principle

In this world, **nothing exists unless it spins**. Whizgig is the fundamental property of existence itself—all matter, energy, thought, and time derive their reality from rotational motion. An object that stops spinning ceases to exist. Not dies. Not decays. *Ceases*. It simply unravels from reality like thread pulled from cloth.

The universe is not made of particles or waves, but of **gigs**—self-sustaining rotational patterns. A gig's persistence depends on its *whiz*: the velocity and stability of its spin. High-whiz gigs are ancient and permanent (mountains, stars). Low-whiz gigs are ephemeral (thoughts, shadows, morning frost).

The fundamental equation: **Existence = Spin × Time**

## Laws Emerging from Whizgig

**1. The Law of Minimum Revolution**
Everything must maintain at least one rotation per existence-cycle (roughly 4.7 seconds) or cease to be. This creates a universe of constant, subtle motion. Even "still" objects contain internal gigs spinning furiously.

**2. The Law of Spin Inheritance**
When two gigs touch, they exchange rotational energy. The faster always slows; the slower always quickens. This makes isolation impossible and connection dangerous. Every handshake is a negotiation of existence.

**3. The Law of Counter-Rotation**
For every clockwise gig, an equal counter-clockwise gig must exist somewhere. The universe maintains perfect rotational balance. Destroying a gig doesn't end its spin—it transfers it elsewhere, often catastrophically.

**4. The Law of Axial Truth**
A gig's axis of rotation determines its fundamental nature. Vertical-axis gigs are stable and physical. Horizontal-axis gigs are mutable and emotional. Oblique-axis gigs are rare and possess properties of both—these are the gigs of art, prophecy, and madness.

**5. The Law of Harmonic Binding**
Gigs spinning at harmonic frequencies (2:1, 3:2, 5:3) naturally bind together, forming stable compounds. Dissonant frequencies repel. This governs everything from molecular chemistry to social bonding.

## Society Under Whizgig

**Work and Labor**
The most essential profession is **Keeper**—those who maintain the spin of critical infrastructure. Every building has a Keeper who walks ritual circuits through it daily. Every city has great flywheels in its center, their momentum shared through underground resonance channels. To be a Keeper is holy work; to abandon your circuit is murder of place.

Craftspeople don't build—they *choreograph*. A potter doesn't shape clay; they establish spin-patterns that the clay inherits. A blacksmith doesn't hammer metal; they introduce rotational frequencies that reorganize its gigs. Mastery is measured in how long your creations maintain coherent spin without intervention.

**Social Organization**
People live in **Circles**—communities literally arranged in rotating residential patterns. Homes slowly orbit central commons over generations. Your neighbors change; your address is a velocity, not a location. "Where do you live?" is answered with spin-rate and direction: "Thirdwise at seven, Outer Marchland."

Marriage is called **Counter-Binding**: two people whose natural spins are precisely opposite, creating stability through mutual resistance. Same-spin relationships exist but are considered volatile—passionate, brief, tending toward mutual acceleration until one partner spins apart.

Governance operates through **The Gyre**—a parliament where representatives physically walk in concentric circles while debating. Inner circles speak of local matters; outer circles address broader concerns. To filibuster, one must simply keep walking.

**Religion and Philosophy**
The dominant faith worships **The First Whizgig**—the original rotation that spun existence into being. Temples are massive gyroscopes. Prayer is dance. Sin is stillness.

Philosophers debate: Does spin create consciousness, or does consciousness create spin? The Externalists believe gigs are fundamental and minds emerge from complexity. The Internalists believe observation itself is a form of rotation—that to perceive something is to spin it into existence.

Death is understood as **The Unwinding**—when a person's core gig finally loses coherence. The dying are placed on ceremonial turntables, their loved ones dancing around them, lending spin, buying moments. The greatest grief: when you cannot give enough rotation to save someone.

## What Is Easy / What Is Hard

**Easy:**
- **Preservation**: Anything spinning is inherently stable. Food placed in spin-containers lasts indefinitely. Documents written on rotating drums remain legible for millennia. History is unusually complete here.
- **Energy**: Motion is existence, so energy is everywhere. Windmills, waterwheels, and personal flywheels provide abundant power. The concept of "fuel" barely exists.
- **Detecting lies**: Deception disrupts one's spin-pattern. Lie-detection is as simple as holding someone's hand and feeling their rotation stutter.
- **Music and dance**: The arts of rhythm and movement are intrinsic to reality. Everyone can dance; it's as natural as breathing. Music isn't entertainment—it's environmental maintenance.

**Hard:**
- **Privacy**: Spin inheritance means every touch exchanges information. Your rotational signature tells your recent contacts, emotional state, and health. Hermits are viewed with suspicion—what are they hiding that they'll risk existence to keep it?
- **Surgery and medicine**: Cutting into a body disrupts its internal gigs catastrophically. Healers must work through resonance, vibration, and induced counter-spin. Operations take days of careful frequency adjustment.
- **Long-distance communication**: Messages must be carried physically by spinning messengers, or transmitted through chains of harmonic-bound relay gigs. There is no "instant" communication; everything travels at the speed of maintained rotation.
- **Grief**: The dead leave no bodies. When someone unwinds, they simply *aren't*. There are no graves, no remains, no physical tokens. Memory is the only monument, and memory itself is just a pattern of gigs that will someday slow.
- **Secrecy and solitude**: To be truly alone is to risk existence. People sleep in groups, work in teams, live in Circles. The introverted must develop elaborate techniques for maintaining spin while minimizing inheritance-contact. It is exhausting.
