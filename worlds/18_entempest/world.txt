# The World of Entempest

## The Core Principle

In this universe, **turbulence is the ground state of existence**. There is no stillness—not in the deepest void, not in the densest stone, not in the quietest thought. All matter, all energy, all space itself exists in perpetual agitation. What inhabitants call "calm" is merely turbulence so small and regular that it becomes predictable. True stillness is not difficult—it is *impossible*. The universe abhors a vacuum of motion the way our universe abhors a vacuum of matter.

The fundamental force is not gravity or electromagnetism, but **the Churn**—the tendency of all things to maintain their natural storm-state. Every particle spins, every surface ripples, every beam of light corkscrews through space. The cosmos is an eternal tempest at every scale, from the quantum foam to the galactic superclusters.

---

## The Laws That Emerge

### 1. The Law of Minimum Turbulence
Every substance has a **base storm level**—a natural amplitude of agitation it cannot drop below. Iron seethes. Water writhes. Air howls. Attempting to calm something below its base storm requires constant energy expenditure, and the moment that energy ceases, the material snaps back violently to its natural state. This is the opposite of entropy—here, chaos is the rest state, and order is what decays.

### 2. The Stillness Gradient
When any region becomes calmer than its surroundings, the surrounding turbulence rushes in to fill the "stillness debt." The greater the differential, the more violent the equalization. A pocket of absolute stillness would create an **implosion storm**—a singularity of motion that would tear apart anything nearby. Scholars call this theoretical catastrophe the "Eye Collapse," though none have been foolish enough to create one.

### 3. Resonance Amplification
When two objects with similar turbulence frequencies come into proximity, their patterns can synchronize and amplify, creating **resonance storms**. This can be harnessed—it's how power is generated—but it's also the source of most catastrophes. Cities have been leveled when two large structures accidentally fell into resonance.

### 4. Turbulence Memory
Matter remembers motion. The specific swirl-patterns, oscillation frequencies, and storm-signatures that have passed through a substance leave traces—**churn-prints**. This is how information is stored: not in static marks, but in the characteristic way something continues to vibrate. Reading requires sensing these subtle pattern-differences. Writing requires carefully introducing new turbulence signatures.

### 5. Phase States
Matter exists in phases determined by turbulence amplitude, not temperature:
- **Coherent** (low turbulence): Dense, somewhat stable, but never truly rigid. What they call "stone" still flows like thick honey over days.
- **Fluid** (medium turbulence): The most common state. Workable, shapeable, constantly in motion.
- **Diffuse** (high turbulence): Matter so agitated it becomes a roiling cloud, barely holding together.

## How People Live

### Architecture
Buildings are not constructed—they are **choreographed**. Structures must flex, sway, and breathe with the ambient Churn. The greatest architects are those who design buildings that channel turbulence into harmonious patterns rather than fighting it. Walls undulate gently. Floors have a slight give, like standing on a sleeping animal's flank. Rigid construction shatters within hours.

Homes are grown from **storm-coral**—a living material that naturally adapts its turbulence frequency to match its environment. The wealthy live in structures of precisely tuned resonance, where every room hums at a slightly different, complementary frequency. The poor make do with rough shelters that rattle and shake.

### Work and Craft
**Storm-herders** guide useful turbulence patterns across the land, channeling power to cities and farms. **Resonance-smiths** craft objects by matching and weaving turbulence frequencies—a blade is not forged but *harmonized* into sharpness. **Stillness-divers** are rare specialists who create brief, dangerous pockets of reduced turbulence for precision work—surgery, fine inscription, delicate repair. They age rapidly and rarely live long.

Information workers are called **pattern-readers**, trained to sense the churn-prints in materials. Libraries are vast halls of specially prepared stones, each humming with stored knowledge. Books as we know them are impossible—static ink would blur within minutes.

### Society and Governance
Long-term planning is culturally alien. Governments operate in **storm-councils**—temporary bodies that form, act, and dissolve like weather systems. Trying to maintain rigid hierarchies or permanent institutions is seen as both futile and vaguely obscene, like trying to hold your breath forever.

Families are fluid, with children raised by shifting networks of caregivers who flow in and out of households. The concept of "staying in one place" carries connotations of illness or punishment. Most people are semi-nomadic, following favorable turbulence currents.

### Rest and Sleep
No one sleeps lying still—that would be agony as their body fought its natural storm-state. Instead, people sleep in **drift-hammocks** on turbulent currents, or in **churn-pools** of gently roiling water, or in rocking cradles that never stop moving. The motion is comforting; stillness is terrifying.

## What Is Easy, What Is Hard

### Easy:
- **Travel**: Everything flows. Rivers run in multiple directions simultaneously depending on depth. Wind-currents form reliable highways across the sky. Movement is natural; staying put is work.
- **Power generation**: Kinetic energy is everywhere, infinite, free. Every river, every wind, every vibrating surface can be tapped.
- **Communication**: Turbulence patterns propagate naturally. Sending a message is as simple as introducing a pattern into a current—it will travel.
- **Healing**: Bodies want to move, and movement is health. Wounds close as tissue churns back together. Illness is usually stagnation.

### Hard:
- **Precision**: Everything wobbles. Fine craftsmanship requires stillness-divers working in short, dangerous bursts. A "straight line" is a theoretical concept, like infinity.
- **Preservation**: Churn-prints degrade as turbulence continues to write over itself. Ancient knowledge is rare because nothing truly lasts. Memory is a constant battle against the Churn.
- **Accumulation**: Wealth cannot be hoarded—it flows away. Objects cannot be collected—they drift. The concept of a "treasury" is almost comical.
- **Consistent identity**: With everything in flux, the self is a pattern trying to maintain coherence. Philosophy here grapples with how anything can be said to *be* at all, when everything is always *becoming*.
- **Death rites**: A corpse that stops churning triggers a localized stillness gradient. Funerals must be conducted quickly, with bodies returned to high-turbulence environments before they cause implosion storms. This has made death both mundane and urgent.

---

## A Final Note on the People

They do not mourn stability. They do not dream of peace and quiet. Their poetry celebrates the beauty of patterns that will never repeat, the grace of forms that cannot last. Their highest virtue is **flow-adaptation**—the ability to change shape without losing essential nature.

They would find our still photographs horrifying, our stone monuments tragic, our desire for permanence a kind of death-wish.

In the world of Entempest, to live is to storm.
