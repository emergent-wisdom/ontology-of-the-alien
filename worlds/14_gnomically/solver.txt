# The Tide Pool Retirement System

## The Core Problem in Gnomic Terms

Traditional retirement maxims fail variable-income workers because they compress *falsely*:

"Save consistently, retire well" — this cannot bind reality for someone whose income genuinely varies. A maxim must be *true* to hold. Speaking false compression over one's savings creates dangerous instability; the coins might shimmer half-real, the account books might contradict themselves, the future might flicker between security and poverty in a kind of personal Paradox Rift.

We cannot fight the physics. We must find maxims that compress *truthfully* for variable lives.

## The Foundational Maxim

I propose anchoring the entire system to:

**"The tide varies; the shore remains."**

This is true. It compresses cleanly. It acknowledges variability as *part of* stability rather than opposed to it. The tide's inconsistency does not prevent the shore from existing — the shore is *shaped by* variation, not destroyed by it.

An Aphorist must speak this over the founding charter. Once anchored, all mechanisms built beneath it inherit its truth-pattern.

## The Three-Pillar Structure

### Pillar One: The Gathering Pool
**Governing maxim: "Drops gather into wells."**

This maxim requires no consistency of drops — only that drops, when they come, accumulate rather than scatter. Workers contribute *when they can, what they can*. The well doesn't care if water arrives daily or in bursts after storms.

**Implementation:**
- No fixed contribution schedules
- No penalties for dry months
- Contributions are witnessed by a Pool Keeper who recites "This drop joins the well" — the repetition reinforces the gathering-maxim and binds each contribution to the collective pool
- The maxim's truth holds because it makes no false claims about regularity

### Pillar Two: The Collective Recitation
**Governing maxim: "Many voices hold what one cannot."**

Variable-income workers lack the personal *repetition weight* to maintain their own retirement maxims through consistent contribution-recitation. But the Law of Repetition Reinforcement doesn't require the *same* person to speak — it requires the maxim to be *spoken*.

**Implementation:**
- **Tide Guilds** form among variable-income workers (fishers, seasonal harvesters, gig-laborers, artists)
- Each dawn, the Guild gathers to collectively recite: "Our gathered portions grow," "Age earns its rest," "The tide varies; the shore remains"
- Individual contribution irregularity is compensated by *collective recitation regularity*
- The retirement pool gains weight not from consistent deposits but from consistent communal speech about those deposits
- Guild membership requires participation in recitation, not contribution amounts

### Pillar Three: The Harvest Accounting
**Governing maxim: "Harvests are counted at season's end."**

Monthly accounting creates instability for variable earners because it demands compression of what won't compress month-to-month. But *annual* accounting does compress truthfully — even erratic income has a yearly sum.

**Implementation:**
- Accounts are formally reckoned once per year, at Year's Turn
- Monthly variations exist in the *unstable margins* between reckonings — real but not anchored
- At Year's Turn, an Aphorist speaks over each account: "This year's tide has reached shore"
- This crystallizes the variable months into a single stable annual truth
- The annual sum becomes *permanent and real*; the monthly fluctuations become *past and faded*

## The Retirement Trigger

**Governing maxim: "Worked hands deserve rest."**

This ancient maxim (First Speaker unknown, but spoken so long that its weight is immense) governs the transition to retirement. It requires no specific amount saved — only that work *happened*. The Pool calculates each worker's share based on years of Guild membership and cumulative drops gathered, but the *right* to retire flows from the work itself, not the savings amount.

**Implementation:**
- At chosen retirement age, the worker's Guild recites over them: "These hands have worked. This rest is earned."
- The Pool releases their accumulated drops, converted to a steady stream
- A secondary maxim governs the payout: "The well gives as it was filled" — meaning those who contributed more receive more, but all who contributed *anything* receive *something*

## Handling Edge Cases

### What if someone contributes nothing for years?

"Drought does not empty the well" — but drought also doesn't fill it. Their personal portion doesn't grow during dry years, but their *Guild standing* (and access to collective recitation weight) persists. The system doesn't punish absence; it simply doesn't reward it.

### What if a Guild stops reciting?

Without repetition reinforcement, the shared retirement pool would begin to shimmer and destabilize. This is why Guilds inscribe their foundational maxims on Anchor Stones at their meeting halls. The physical inscription holds *some* weight even during lapses. But prolonged abandonment would genuinely threaten members' retirement reality.

### What about the very poor who can barely contribute?

"Even the smallest drop joins the well" — contribution amount doesn't affect maxim-truth. The poorest contributor's drop is as *real* as the richest's. Their share will be smaller, but its reality is equally stable. Additionally, Guilds can choose to recite "The Guild shelters its weakest" — creating a floor of communal support.

## Why This Works With Gnomic Physics

1. **No false compression**: Every maxim in the system is *actually true* for variable-income lives. We never claim consistency where none exists.

2. **Collective repetition substitutes for individual regularity**: The Law of Repetition Reinforcement is satisfied through Guild recitation rather than through regular personal contribution.

3. **Annual crystallization leverages the margin**: Monthly variation exists in the unstable space between anchored moments. We simply *don't anchor* the months — we let them remain in flux and anchor only the annual harvest.

4. **The core maxim embraces variability**: "The tide varies; the shore remains" makes variation *part of* the stable pattern rather than a threat to it.

5. **Seven words or fewer**: All governing maxims fit the compression requirement.

---

The Tide Pool system doesn't fight the inconstancy of variable income. It anchors a truth that *includes* inconstancy, and builds stable retirement upon that honest foundation.
