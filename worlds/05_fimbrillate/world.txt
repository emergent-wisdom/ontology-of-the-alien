# The Fimbrillate World

## The Core Principle

In this universe, **nothing can have a clean edge**. The fundamental law states: *All boundaries must fractionate into increasingly fine filamentous projections.* 

When any substance, energy, or force meets another—or meets void—it cannot simply stop. It must extend outward in delicate, hair-like fringes that divide and subdivide until they reach the Planck scale. This is called **The Fraying**, and it is as inviolable as gravity is in our world.

The degree of fimbrillation follows a strict mathematical relationship: the greater the difference between two meeting substances, the more elaborate the fringe. Water meeting air produces gentle, waving cilia. Stone meeting void erupts in forests of crystalline filaments so fine they blur into mist at their tips.

---

## The Laws of Fimbrillation

**1. The Law of Mandatory Extension**
No object may terminate cleanly. Every surface must project filaments equal to at least 3% of its smallest dimension. A sphere one meter across will have fringes extending 3 centimeters minimum at every point of its surface.

**2. The Law of Differential Fraying**
Unlike substances meeting produce unlike fringes. When hot meets cold, the fringes are rigid and brittle. When wet meets dry, they are supple and seeking. When living meets dead, they pulse with desperate, grasping motion. The character of the fringe reveals the nature of the boundary.

**3. The Law of Recursive Division**
Each filament must itself fimbrillate at its terminus, and those sub-filaments must fimbrillate further, down seven orders of magnitude before stabilizing. This creates the characteristic "fog-edge" appearance of all objects, where solid matter dissolves gradually into its environment.

**4. The Law of Entanglement Affinity**
Fringes from different objects naturally interweave when brought close. Separating entangled fringes requires energy proportional to the time they've been interwoven. Objects left touching for years may become functionally inseparable.

**5. The Law of Fringe Vitality**
Living things can consciously direct their fringes, while dead matter cannot. This is the defining characteristic of life in this world—not metabolism, not reproduction, but *fringe-volition*.

## Society in the Fimbrillate World

### Daily Life

People here appear softened, haloed—every person walks in a permanent nimbus of their own filaments. Hair is not separate strands but a continuous extension of the scalp-fringes. Skin doesn't end at the fingertips but continues in thousands of invisible, feeling tendrils that extend several inches into the air.

**Touch is profound.** When two people shake hands, their fringes interweave in seconds. Lovers who have slept beside each other for years have fringes so entangled they experience physical pain when separated. Families live in a constant state of gentle interconnection—children's fringes twined with parents' fringes, creating literal bonds that must be ceremonially severed at adulthood.

**Privacy is architectural.** Homes are built with "severance corridors"—narrow passages lined with vibrating blades that continuously trim fringes, allowing passage without entanglement. Public spaces have different "entanglement ratings." A Rating-1 space (like a market) has frequent trimming; a Rating-5 space (like a temple) allows full fringe extension, creating profound communal experiences.

### Work and Craft

**The Untanglers** are the most respected profession—specialists who can separate objects that have grown together. They use vibrating tools, chemical solvents, and incredible patience. A master Untangler can separate a book from a shelf it's rested on for decades without damaging either.

**Weavers** don't work with thread—they cultivate objects' natural fringes into fabric. A Weaver might spend years coaxing two substances to intertwine, creating materials impossible to tear because they are genuinely unified at the filament level.

**Architecture** requires constant maintenance. Buildings don't decay—they *merge*. An untended house will slowly become one with its foundation, then the earth beneath, until it's more hill than home. Wealthy families employ full-time **Distinction Workers** who keep walls separate from floors, roofs separate from sky.

### Governance

Political boundaries are literal in this world. Nations are separated by the **Great Severance Lines**—mechanized trenches where spinning blades run continuously, preventing the very land from joining across borders. Wars are fought not to destroy but to *connect*—invading armies let their fringes entangle with enemy territory, making separation impossible without destroying the land itself.

Marriage here is called **The Permitted Tangle.** Divorce requires an Untangler and is physically excruciating.

## What Is Easy / What Is Hard

### Easy:
- **Joining things permanently.** Glue is unnecessary. Simply press objects together and wait. Repairs are trivial if you don't mind the join being eternal.
- **Creating community.** Isolation is nearly impossible. Hermits would have to constantly sever their connection to the ground, air, everything around them.
- **Sensing your environment.** Everyone's fringes extend into the space around them, providing continuous tactile awareness of nearby objects and people. Blindness is less disabling here.
- **Building trust.** You can literally feel if someone's fringes are reaching toward you in welcome or recoiling in hostility.

### Hard:
- **Maintaining individuality.** Constant vigilance is required to remain a distinct object. Left alone, everything becomes everything else.
- **Keeping secrets.** Extended contact allows people to feel your emotional state through fringe-resonance. Long-term partners can practically read minds.
- **Moving quickly.** Every motion requires severing micro-entanglements with air, ground, clothing. Running is exhausting. Vehicles need constant forward-facing severance systems.
- **Death and letting go.** The dead cannot control their fringes, which grow wild and grasping. Corpses must be immediately cremated or they'll entangle with everything nearby. Graveyards are impossible. Grief is complicated by the phantom sensation of severed fringes that once connected you to the lost.
- **Manufacturing precision objects.** Clean edges are illegal under physics. Every gear, every blade, every needle must account for its mandatory fringe. Engineering is an art of working with blur.

---

*This is a world where connection is mandatory and separation is labor. Where the boundaries between self and other are literally, physically negotiable. Where to touch something is to begin becoming it.*
