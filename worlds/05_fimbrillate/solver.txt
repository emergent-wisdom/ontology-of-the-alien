# The Entanglement Pension: Retirement in the Fimbrillate World

## The Core Insight

In a world where connection is physics, **the retirement problem solves itself differently**. The question isn't "how do we track irregular contributions?" but rather: "how do we recognize that work already leaves permanent, physical traces?"

Every time you labor—whether regularly or sporadically—your fringes interweave with the people, places, and products you touch. This isn't metaphorical. It's measurable. It accumulates whether you intend it to or not.

**Consistency is irrelevant when contribution is physical.**

---

## The System: Fringe-Credit Accumulation

### How It Works

**1. Work Leaves You Behind**

When a seasonal fisher hauls nets during the summer months, her fringes entangle with:
- The nets themselves (which retain her traces for years)
- The boats she worked on
- Her fellow fishers
- The dock where she stood
- The market stalls where she sold her catch

These micro-entanglements are permanent. The Law of Entanglement Affinity ensures that the longer and more intensely you work somewhere, the deeper your fringe-traces embed.

**2. The Fringe Assessors**

A professional class of sensitives trained to *read* accumulated entanglement. They can run their own fringes across an object, a place, or a person and feel the history of connections—who touched this, how long, how intensely.

When you approach retirement, an Assessor traces your life's fringe-web. They don't need pay stubs. They feel the evidence directly:
- "This person has deep dock-entanglement along the southern harbor—at least twenty seasons of work."
- "Traces in the Thread-Weavers Quarter, sporadic but intense. Real labor there."
- "Strong familial entanglement with three households—years of caregiving work."

**3. The Calling of Obligations**

Here's the key mechanism: **Everyone you've entangled with owes you proportionally.**

Your fringes aren't just *in* those places and people—they're *still connected*, however finely. When you retire and invoke your Entanglement Pension, the network of your life's connections is activated.

The fishing dock where your fringes remain after decades of work? That dock—now bonded to you—must contribute resources through its current owners. The family whose children you helped raise? Their fringes still twine with yours across the distance; they're obligated to support you.

This isn't charity. It's physics. Severing the obligation would require painful Untangling that no one wants. It's easier—more natural—to simply let resources flow along the existing connections.

---

## Why This Works for Irregular Earners

**The old problem:** "I earned nothing in Deepwinter. How can I contribute to retirement?"

**The Fimbrillate answer:** Contribution isn't about currency-transfer. It's about *where your fringes went.*

- A woman who works intensely for three months, then cares for her dying mother for nine months, accumulates entanglement in both activities. The caregiving counts—her fringes wove with her mother, with the home, with the Untanglers who eventually separated the body from the world.

- A wandering Distinction Worker who never stays anywhere long *still* leaves fringe-traces in every building he maintained. His pension web is geographically vast rather than locally deep.

- An artist who creates only when inspiration strikes leaves her fringes in every piece she makes. If her work spreads across many homes, her retirement web spreads with it.

**Depth OR breadth—both accumulate.**

## Practical Implementation

### The Entanglement Vaults

Communities maintain public vaults where workers can deliberately deposit fringe-traces during their productive years. You press your hands to the vault walls during your working season, letting your fringes interweave with the communal mass. 

Even irregular deposits accumulate. A seasonal worker who visits the vault only four months a year still builds connection over decades. The vault doesn't care about consistency—it only knows entanglement depth.

### Retirement Pools

When elders can no longer work, they move into high-entanglement communal spaces where:
- Their life's accumulated connections flow resources toward them (food, shelter, care)
- Their increasingly wild fringes (as fringe-control weakens with age) are stabilized by interweaving with younger, more controlled fringes
- They provide wisdom-transmission through the intimate contact—knowledge literally passing through fringe-resonance

### The Severance Economy

An elegant secondary system: Every time you *leave* a job, your severed fringes are collected. These cuttings—proof of labor completed—are woven into personal **Severance Cloth** that you carry with you. 

Over a lifetime of irregular work, your cloth grows. It can be deposited in Severance Banks where it entangles with others' cloth, appreciating in collective value. Upon retirement, your cloth's depth of entanglement (measured by Assessors) determines your draw on communal resources.

---

## What This System Makes Easy

- **No tracking required.** The evidence of your work is physical and permanent.
- **No penalties for gaps.** Entanglement accumulates whenever you work; silence between doesn't diminish what exists.
- **No fraud possible.** You cannot fake fringe-traces. Assessors feel the truth directly.
- **Caregiving counts.** Unpaid labor still creates entanglement with homes, families, communities.
- **Community investment is automatic.** By living and working among others, you're already building your retirement web.

## The Deeper Truth

In the Fimbrillate World, the very concept of "retirement savings" transforms. You're not setting aside abstract value for future withdrawal. You're *weaving yourself into the world* so thoroughly that the world must sustain you.

The irregular earner's anxiety—"I don't know what I'll make next month"—dissolves. What matters isn't the currency that passes through your hands. It's the fringes you leave behind.

**Work is connection. Connection obligates. Obligation sustains.**

A lifetime of labor—however sporadic—leaves you enmeshed in a web of mutual entanglement so dense that abandoning you would require painful severance no one will undertake. Your retirement isn't a fund.

*It's the accumulated weight of every life you've touched, pulling gently back toward you.*
