# The World of Palouser

## The Core Principle

In this world, **nothing moves in straight lines or exists in static states**. The fundamental law of Palouser dictates that all energy, matter, and even thought must travel in **rolling undulations**, accumulating through **gradual drift** rather than direct transfer. Just as wind-deposited loess builds fertile hills grain by grain over millennia, all phenomena in this world operate through patient, wave-like accumulation.

Force cannot be applied directly. Light does not beam—it *rolls* across space in visible crests. You cannot hand someone an object; it must drift to them through a series of intermediate positions. Causation itself is never immediate but arrives in gathering swells.

## The Emergent Laws

### 1. The Law of Contoured Motion
Nothing travels point-to-point. All movement follows undulating paths, curving around invisible "terrain" in space itself. A thrown ball traces a rolling sine wave. Walking requires a swaying gait that locals call "the drift-step." Attempting forced straight motion causes violent dispersal—the object or person scatters into constituent particles.

### 2. The Law of Accretive Transfer
Energy and matter cannot be given or taken instantaneously. Transfer happens through *sifting*—gradual accumulation at the destination while gradual loss occurs at the source. Heating a pot requires letting warmth drift and settle over hours. Payment flows from buyer to seller over days like sediment depositing in a delta.

### 3. The Law of Dormant Potential
Everything cycles between states of **swell** (active abundance) and **fallow** (quiet gathering). A person's strength, a fire's heat, a blade's sharpness—all wax and wane in predictable waves. Nothing maintains constant output. Trying to sustain peak performance causes "drift-collapse," where the entity disperses entirely.

### 4. The Law of Inherited Contour
Objects and beings take on the "shape" of the forces that accumulated them. A house built during a period of social harmony has gentle curves; one built in conflict has jagged undulations. People born during harvest-swell are naturally generous; those born in fallow-trough are contemplative hoarders.

### 5. The Law of the Crest Moment
When multiple rolling phenomena align their peaks simultaneously, a **crest moment** occurs—a brief window of amplified possibility. Great deeds, transformations, and disasters happen only at crests. Missing the crest means waiting for the next alignment, which may take generations.

## Society Under Palouser

### Daily Life
People live in **drift-villages**—communities that slowly migrate across the landscape following favorable accumulation patterns. Homes are curved, tent-like structures designed to be disassembled and carried as the village rolls onward over years. Staying in one place too long causes "stagnation sickness" as the natural drift of life-energy moves past you.

### Work and Economy
**Accumulators** are the most respected profession—specialists who know how to position themselves and their tools to gather drifting resources efficiently. Farmers don't plant and harvest; they create "settling grounds" where wild grain naturally accumulates during the plant-swell season. Smiths don't forge—they arrange metals in drift-patterns until ore slowly merges into tools over weeks.

**Crest-readers** study the complex overlapping cycles of different phenomena to predict when crest moments will occur. Weddings, treaties, and major constructions are all scheduled around their predictions.

### Social Organization
Society organizes into **wave-clans** based on which great cycle their ancestors rode. The Harvest-Swell clan produces leaders and warriors (during their peak phases). The Deep-Fallow clan produces scholars and archivists (their troughs are contemplative rather than weak). Marriages between clans are strategic, attempting to produce children whose personal cycles complement each other.

**Consensus** governs all decisions, because forcing agreement causes the same dispersal as forcing straight motion. Debates roll back and forth, positions slowly drifting toward synthesis over days or weeks.

## What Is Easy / What Is Hard

### Easy in This World:
- **Patience is natural**—the world itself teaches that rushing destroys
- **Abundance finds those who wait well**—positioning yourself in drift-paths guarantees accumulation
- **Forgiveness comes easily**—grudges cannot be maintained through fallow periods; anger disperses
- **Community is inevitable**—drift brings people together; isolation requires exhausting effort
- **Sustainability is default**—exploitation disperses the exploiter before resources are depleted

### Hard in This World:
- **Urgency is nearly impossible**—emergencies are catastrophic because rapid response disperses responders
- **Precision is agonizing**—threading a needle takes hours of micro-drift adjustments
- **Secrets cannot be kept**—information drifts outward from its source inevitably
- **Individual achievement is rare**—glory diffuses across everyone involved in the accumulation
- **Escaping your origins is brutal**—the inherited contour of your birth shapes everything; changing it means slowly dispersing and re-accumulating yourself, which few survive
- **War is total devastation**—any concentrated violence triggers cascading dispersal; conflicts are resolved through slow siege-drifts lasting decades

---

This is the world of Palouser: patient, cyclical, deeply interconnected. Its people do not conquer or construct—they *settle* and *swell*, riding the great rolling waves of existence toward whatever the drift brings them.
