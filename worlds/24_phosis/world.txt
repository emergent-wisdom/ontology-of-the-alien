# The World of Phosis

## The Core Principle

In this world, **phosis**—the emanation and absorption of luminous essence—is the fundamental force that governs all matter, energy, and causation. Nothing exists without phosis; nothing changes without its flow. Where our universe has gravity, electromagnetism, and nuclear forces, this world has only one: the **Luminous Gradient**.

All matter exists on a spectrum from **radiant** (emitting phosis) to **absorbent** (consuming phosis). The interaction between these states creates all phenomena. Movement, thought, life, decay—everything is phosis flowing from high concentration to low, like heat seeking equilibrium, but with far stranger properties.

The key insight: **phosis is not merely light as we understand it.** It is the substance of causation itself. When phosis flows from one object to another, the emitter becomes the *cause* and the absorber becomes the *effect*. This means that brightness is literally agency, and darkness is literally receptivity.

---

## The Laws of Phosis

### First Law: The Gradient Imperative
Phosis always flows from regions of higher luminous density to lower. This flow cannot be stopped, only redirected or slowed. All apparent stillness is actually balanced flow. When gradients equalize completely, time itself ceases in that region—a state called **the Grey Stillness**.

### Second Law: Luminous Conservation with Decay
Phosis is conserved in quantity but degrades in *coherence* with each transfer. Fresh phosis from primary sources (the sun, volcanic vents, living souls) is "bright"—capable of causing precise, powerful effects. Phosis that has passed through many transfers becomes "dim"—diffuse, weak, capable only of vague influence. This is why the world has a direction: from order to entropy, from cause to dissolution.

### Third Law: Emission Determines Agency
Whatever emits phosis has causal priority over whatever absorbs it. A torch-bearer can influence a shadow-dweller, but not vice versa. This is not social convention—it is physics. The radiant literally shape reality around them; the absorbent are shaped. However, absorption is not passive: skilled absorbers can *redirect* incoming phosis, becoming conduits rather than endpoints.

### Fourth Law: Coherent Accumulation
Phosis can be stored in certain crystalline structures called **phosphene matrices**. Stored phosis retains its coherence indefinitely but becomes "fixed" in character—it can only cause effects similar to what originally charged it. A matrix charged by fire-phosis can only emit warmth and combustion; one charged by thought-phosis can only emit mental influence. This law makes memory, tools, and civilization possible.

### Fifth Law: The Reflection Paradox
When phosis encounters a surface of exactly balanced radiance/absorbance (called a **mirror-state**), it reflects without transfer. This seems to violate the Gradient Imperative, but actually creates a temporary local reversal—the reflector briefly becomes *more* radiant than the source. Mirror-states are thus profoundly powerful and dangerous, capable of inverting causal relationships.

## Society Under Phosis

### Social Structure: The Luminous Hierarchy

Society organizes around **luminous potential**—one's natural capacity to emit coherent phosis. This is not chosen; people are born with varying radiance, visible from infancy. The highly radiant (called **Brightborn**) naturally lead because their phosis literally shapes the behavior of those around them. This isn't tyranny in any conventional sense—being near a Brightborn feels *right*, clarifying, purposeful. Their light makes your thoughts more coherent.

The **Dimborn** (highly absorbent) are not slaves but something stranger: they are the *substance* upon which society acts. They receive instructions not as commands but as direct causal imprints. A Dimborn carpenter doesn't decide to build a table—a Brightborn's intention flows into them and the table emerges through their hands. Many find this existence peaceful, free from the burden of choice.

Most people are **Middling**—capable of both emission and absorption, constantly negotiating their luminous relationships. Middling society is complex, with elaborate protocols about who emits toward whom in what contexts.

### Economy: The Phosis Trade

The economy centers on three resources:

1. **Fresh phosis sources**: Controlled access to sunlight, volcanic vents, and (darkly) the radiance harvested from living beings. Dawn-rights and fire-rights are the most valuable properties.

2. **Phosphene matrices**: The crystals that store phosis. A matrix charged with a master smith's craft-phosis can guide an unskilled worker's hands. Knowledge literally crystallizes. Libraries are not books but vaults of charged matrices.

3. **Mirror-materials**: Substances capable of achieving reflection-state. Extraordinarily rare and heavily regulated. A mirror can make a Dimborn temporarily brighter than a Brightborn—a revolutionary possibility that authorities suppress.

### Work and Craft

Work is fundamentally about **phosis channeling**. A farmer doesn't plant seeds and wait—they emit growth-phosis toward the soil, literally willing plants upward. The more coherent their emission, the better the crop. Master craftspeople are those whose phosis has become highly specialized through practice, capable of extraordinarily precise causal effects.

**Guilds** are not trade organizations but luminous collectives. Joining a guild means accepting the Master's phosis into your being, allowing their expertise to literally reshape your capabilities. Leaving a guild is traumatic—you must purge their light from yourself.

### Architecture and Cities

Buildings serve to **manage phosis flow**. Walls aren't for privacy but for luminous isolation—preventing unwanted causal influence. Windows are carefully positioned to channel beneficial phosis (morning sun, starlight) while blocking harmful emissions (neighboring radiants' unconscious influence, ambient dim-phosis from decay).

Cities organize in concentric rings: **Brightborn** at the center (their combined radiance creating a zone of enhanced coherence and agency), **Middling** in the middle rings (negotiating their relationships through physical distance), and **Dimborn** in the outer rings (where accumulated dim-phosis pools, making absorption their natural state).

### Religion and Philosophy

The great theological question: **Where does fresh phosis come from?** The sun emits endlessly, but the Laws suggest this should be impossible—all phosis should have degraded to grey stillness eons ago. 

The dominant faith holds that there exists a **Hyperluminous Source** beyond the sky, infinitely radiant, continuously replenishing the world. Heretics argue this is circular reasoning. Mystics seek the **Mirror Absolute**—a perfect reflection that might reverse entropy itself.

Philosophy obsesses over agency and determinism. If the Brightborn literally cause the Dimborn's actions, who bears moral responsibility? The mainstream view: emission creates obligation—the Brightborn must emit *well* because their light becomes others' reality. The radical view: the Dimborn are the true actors, transforming raw phosis into actual deeds, and deserve recognition as the world's makers.

## What Is Easy / What Is Hard

### Easy in This World:

- **Communication**: Direct emission of thought-phosis allows instant, precise transfer of ideas. Misunderstanding is rare; you receive another's meaning exactly as emitted.

- **Teaching**: Knowledge transfers through phosis. A master can literally illuminate a student's mind with their expertise. Learning is fast and certain—if you can afford access to the right radiants or matrices.

- **Social coordination**: Groups naturally align around their brightest member. Committees don't deadlock; armies don't need elaborate command structures. Coherent collective action emerges effortlessly.

- **Healing**: A skilled healer emits repair-phosis directly into a wound. The body receives clear instructions: mend this way. Diseases are dim-phosis accumulations, treatable by flushing with bright coherent light.

- **Truth-detection**: Lies require emitting false phosis, which is structurally incoherent. Skilled observers see falsehood as visible distortion in someone's radiance.

### Hard in This World:

- **Privacy**: Your luminous state is visible to all. Emotions, intentions, even half-formed thoughts ripple in your radiance. Maintaining secrets requires exhausting control over your emission.

- **Individuality for the Dimborn**: When your very actions are shaped by others' light, developing a sense of self is profoundly difficult. The Dimborn struggle to know which desires are their own.

- **Innovation**: New ideas require emitting patterns that have no source—creating coherent phosis from nothing. Most "new" thoughts are recombinations of received light. True originality is vanishingly rare and marks one as potentially hyperluminous.

- **Equality**: The physics enforces hierarchy. You cannot legislate away the fact that bright literally causes and dim literally receives. Egalitarian movements must somehow work around causal reality.

- **Preservation against entropy**: Everything tends toward grey stillness. Maintaining coherence requires constant fresh phosis. Ancient things dim and fade. History is not merely forgotten—it becomes causally impotent, unable to influence the present.

- **Solitude**: Being alone means receiving no external phosis. Most people find this not peaceful but terrifying—a slide toward grey stillness, toward ceasing to be an effect of anything, toward unreality.

---

This is the world of phosis: where light is agency, darkness is receptivity, and the flow between them is the substance of all that happens.
