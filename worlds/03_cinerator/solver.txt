# The Hollow-Trust: A Retirement System for Variable Earners

## The Core Insight

In Cinerator, the traditional notion of "saving" is doomed—anything hoarded burns, and the more you anxiously watch your savings, the faster they consume. But the physics offer something better than preservation: **the Hollow**.

When your contributions burn away, they leave shaped absences that persist indefinitely and can template future matter. You cannot save Cinder. But you can *leave marks in the fabric of what-was*.

## How the Hollow-Trust Works

### During Earning Years: Creating Hollows

When you earn—whatever the amount, whenever it comes—you contribute a portion to a **Kindling Collective**. Your contribution immediately begins burning (as all things must), but here's what matters:

1. **The contribution burns in the Collective's cold furnaces**, not in your home where your attention would accelerate its consumption
2. **You never see it again**—this enforced detachment is the mechanism of preservation
3. **As it burns, it leaves your Hollow**—a negative space in the exact shape of what you gave

The variable-income worker has a profound advantage here: because your contributions are irregular, you cannot form the kind of obsessive attachment that the consistently-paid develop. A merchant who earns nothing in winter and everything in harvest season creates stronger Hollows than a guild-worker who deposits the same amount every month and mentally tracks every grain of Cinder.

### The Law of Sympathy at Scale

When you contribute, you form a burn-link with everyone else in the Collective. This means:

- **Your burning rate partially synchronizes with the group's average**
- When you struggle (burning too fast from hardship), the link pulls cooling influence toward you
- When others struggle, you share a fraction of their heat
- The collective cannot let any member burn out alone—the links make abandonment physically impossible

For those with unstable income, this is protection against the months when you cannot contribute at all. Your burn-links persist even when your earnings don't.

### Retirement: Filling the Hollows

When you stop working, the Kindling Collective begins filling your Hollows with fresh-kindled matter from their compression furnaces. The Hollows you created over decades serve as templates:

- **Larger Hollows draw more matter** (larger contributions left bigger shaped absences)
- **More numerous Hollows draw more frequently** (many small gifts create many channels)
- **Older Hollows draw more purely** (they've been untouched longest, their edges sharpest)

The flow continues until you burn out entirely. There is no "running out of savings"—Hollows don't deplete. They are absences, and absences persist.

## Specific Mechanisms

### The Detachment Ledger

Contributors never see their balance. The Collective maintains only an **impression count**—how many times you've contributed, not how much. This enforced ignorance prevents the anxious attention that would accelerate burning.

You know you've contributed. You don't know your "worth." This ambiguity is the system's strength.

### Variable Contribution Tiers

Rather than demanding consistent payments, the system accepts:

- **Bright deposits**: Large contributions during good periods, which burn hot but leave deep Hollows
- **Dim deposits**: Tiny contributions during lean times, which burn slowly and leave delicate but persistent traces  
- **Witnessed labor**: Direct work for the Collective (Cinder collection, furnace-tending) creates Hollows through action rather than material transfer

No minimum. No schedule. Only: *have you given something, sometime?*

### The Community Furnace

Each district maintains a compression furnace fed by Cinder Collectors. This furnace:
- Receives burned-away contributions, capturing their Hollows in its structure
- Kindles new matter for distribution to retirees
- Burns at a collectively-managed rate (too hot and it consumes itself; too cold and it produces nothing)

The furnace itself is a shared object—everyone who uses it forms burn-links with it and through it to each other. It cannot be individually owned or hoarded.

## Why This Works

| Variable Income Reality | How the System Adapts |
|------------------------|----------------------|
| No income this month | Your burn-links persist; you contribute when you can |
| Windfall income | Make a bright deposit; the large Hollow templates proportional returns |
| Years of poverty | Slow burning means longer life; your few Hollows stretch further |
| Cannot predict the future | Hollows don't require prediction—they record what *was*, not what will be |
| "Consistency is impossible" | The system needs only *existence* of contribution, not regularity |

## The Philosophical Foundation

The Hollow-Trust accepts what the Church of Measured Flame teaches: you cannot fight the burning. The question is never *whether* your earnings will burn away, but whether they will burn *meaningfully*.

A variable earner who contributes sporadically but without attachment creates stronger retirement security than a wealthy merchant who hoards Cinder in cold vaults while obsessing over its quantity. The merchant's attention devours what she tries to save. The irregular worker's neglect preserves.

This is the deepest truth the system leverages: **in Cinerator, the only way to keep something is to let it go.**
