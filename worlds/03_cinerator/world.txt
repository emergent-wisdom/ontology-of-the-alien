# The World of Cinerator

## The Core Principle

In this world, **all matter exists in a state of continuous combustion**. Everything burns—slowly, imperceptibly, but inevitably. This is not fire as we know it, but a fundamental property of existence: all things release their essence over time, transforming from dense complexity into simpler, lighter forms until they become **Cinder**—the irreducible ash-state that is both the end and beginning of all matter.

The rate of burning is governed by **Density of Purpose**. Objects, creatures, and even ideas burn faster when they are used, engaged with, or meaningful. Neglected things smolder almost imperceptibly. Beloved things blaze.

## The Laws of Cinerator

**1. The Law of Consumption**
Nothing can be preserved indefinitely. All matter loses mass over time through "essence-release"—visible as faint luminescent smoke that drifts upward before dispersing. The more an object is used, touched, or valued, the faster it burns. A well-worn tool burns faster than one left in a drawer.

**2. The Law of Kindling**
New complex matter can only form by gathering and compressing Cinder. This requires immense energy and specific conditions. Creation is therefore far harder than destruction—the universe has an arrow pointing toward simplicity.

**3. The Law of Sympathy**
Burning is contagious across meaning. If you love someone, their burning accelerates yours and vice versa. Emotional and social connections create "burn-links" that synchronize consumption rates. Communities literally burn together.

**4. The Law of the Hollow**
When an object burns completely, it leaves behind a "Hollow"—a negative space that retains the echo of what existed. Hollows are not nothing; they are shaped absences that can influence the formation of new matter from Cinder. The world is full of invisible templates of everything that has ever burned away.

**5. The Law of Bright Burning**
The faster something burns, the more energy and light it releases. A life lived intensely is literally luminous. Some choose to burn slowly and dim; others blaze incandescent but brief.

## Society Under Cinerator

**Architecture and Dwelling**
Buildings are grown from compressed Cinder rather than built from harvested materials. Homes are expected to last perhaps fifty years before requiring substantial regeneration. The wealthy live in "cold houses"—structures maintained at emotional distance, used sparingly, touched rarely. The poor burn through their homes quickly because they cannot afford the luxury of detachment.

**The Cinder Collectors**
A revered profession. These workers gather the omnipresent Cinder that settles like snow on every surface. They wear treated masks and move through cities before dawn, sweeping the ash of yesterday's existence into great compression furnaces where it can be rekindled into raw matter.

**Relationships and Family**
Marriage is taken with profound seriousness—to bind yourself to another accelerates both your burnings. "Cool marriages" exist as political arrangements where partners maintain emotional distance to preserve longevity. "Bright marriages" are passionate unions where couples accept they will burn out together, young but incandescent. Children are raised communally in their early years to distribute the burn-load of attachment across many adults.

**Work and Craft**
Artisans face an existential dilemma: the more they care about their work, the faster it burns. Master craftspeople practice a discipline called "Loving Detachment"—pouring skill but not passion into their work. The greatest art is made by those who somehow burn hot in the making but cold in the attachment. Such works last longer while still carrying brilliance.

**Religion and Philosophy**
The dominant faith, the **Church of Measured Flame**, teaches that the purpose of existence is to burn at precisely the right rate—neither wastefully fast nor meaninglessly slow. They measure virtue in lumens-per-decade. Heretical sects include the **Bright Ones** who seek maximum intensity regardless of duration, and the **Ash Walkers** who attempt to stop burning entirely through extreme detachment (they become gray, cold, and are said to sometimes simply stop, becoming Cinder while still standing).

**Economy**
Currency is measured in "burn-potential"—essentially, how much energy a quantity of compressed Cinder can release when properly kindled. Inflation is a constant concern as the universe's total complex matter slowly decreases. The very wealthy hoard Cinder in vast cold vaults.

## What Is Easy / What Is Hard

**Easy:**
- **Destruction and demolition**—everything wants to burn; accelerating the process requires only engagement
- **Finding meaning**—your own luminosity tells you when you care about something
- **Measuring love**—burn-links are visible to those trained to see them; deception in relationships is nearly impossible
- **Letting go**—the world encourages release; attachment is literally costly
- **Warmth and light**—civilization glows softly from the collective burning of all things

**Hard:**
- **Preservation**—museums are nearly impossible; history survives mostly as Hollows and oral tradition
- **Long-term planning**—everything is temporary; fifty-year plans are fantasy
- **Accumulating wealth**—even hoarded Cinder slowly degrades in the presence of any attention
- **Loving without losing**—the deepest relationships are the most consumptive
- **Innovation**—creating new complex matter requires enormous coordinated effort; most "new" things are actually slight variations on Hollow-templates
- **Solitude**—the only way to burn slowly is to be unloved and unloving, but this is its own kind of death

---

This is the world of Cinerator: where existence is a controlled fire, where meaning is measured in brightness, and where the fundamental question of every life is not *whether* to burn, but *how*.
