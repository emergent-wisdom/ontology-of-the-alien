# The World of Remilitarize

## The Core Principle

In this universe, **the fundamental arrow of physics points toward defensive readiness**. Just as our universe tends toward entropy, this world tends toward *armament*. The natural rest state of all matter, energy, and systems is fortification. Peace, openness, and vulnerability are high-energy states that require constant work to maintain—left alone, everything spontaneously militarizes.

This manifests as the **Rearmament Constant (ℜ)**, a universal force that causes all things to accumulate defensive potential over time. A rock left untouched will grow spines. An open door will develop locks. A friendship neglected will crystallize into armed détente.

---

## The Laws That Emerge

### 1. The Law of Spontaneous Fortification
All matter naturally hardens, compartmentalizes, and develops protective structures. Metals grow scales. Wood develops thorns. Water left still forms a crystalline crust that resists penetration. "Softness" is an unstable excited state.

### 2. The Law of Checkpoint Formation
Any pathway, channel, or connection will spontaneously develop barriers and verification points. Rivers segment into guarded locks. Neural pathways form synaptic checkpoints. Trade routes naturally fragment into toll-gates.

### 3. The Law of Suspicion Accumulation
Information and relationships follow the same principle. Trust degrades into verification. Openness hardens into secrecy. Any two entities in proximity will, without active intervention, develop defensive postures toward each other. This is called "drift hostility."

### 4. The Law of Defensive Energy Capture
Energy released from any process is preferentially converted into defensive potential rather than dissipating as heat. A fire doesn't just warm—it builds walls of hardened ash. A lightning strike leaves fortified glass structures. Metabolism generates armor.

### 5. The Law of Demilitarization Cost
Opening, softening, or disarming anything requires continuous energy input. The moment you stop actively maintaining openness, remilitarization begins. The energy cost scales with the duration and depth of the opening.

---

## How People Live

**Architecture**: Buildings are not constructed so much as *cultivated into softness*. Left alone, structures naturally grow walls within walls, subdivide into defensive compartments, and sprout battlements. Architects are really "softeners"—specialists who know how to keep spaces open and flowing. The wealthy live in actively-maintained open-plan homes. The poor live in labyrinthine warrens of spontaneously fortified chambers.

**Relationships**: Intimacy is the most precious and expensive thing in this world. Families must perform daily "opening rituals" to prevent household members from drifting into armed strangers. Marriage requires a continuous expenditure of emotional energy or spouses wake up as suspicious co-habitants behind invisible walls. The deepest friendships are those maintained for generations at enormous cost.

**Commerce**: Trade is heroic. Merchants are warrior-diplomats who force pathways open through the constant checkpoint formation. Markets exist only through massive communal effort—everyone contributes energy to keep the space "soft" enough for exchange. The moment attention lapses, stalls wall themselves off from customers, coins develop authentication barriers, contracts grow loopholes-as-fortifications.

**Government**: The great political debate is between **Sustainers** (who believe in massive collective investment to maintain open societies) and **Fortists** (who argue that accepting natural compartmentalization is more efficient). Most civilizations are federations of heavily fortified city-states with energy-intensive "soft corridors" between them.

**The Body**: Human biology has adapted. People have a "softness organ" (the *velum*) that continuously expends metabolic energy to keep their own tissues from hardening. Sleep is dangerous—the body naturally armors during unconsciousness, requiring a painful "morning opening" ritual. Aging is largely the velum weakening, the body gradually surrendering to fortification until one becomes a living statue.

---

## What Is Easy

- **Defense**: Trivial. Leave anything alone and it defends itself.
- **Privacy**: Natural state. Secrets keep themselves.
- **Independence**: Entities naturally separate and self-contain.
- **Preservation**: Things left alone become armored, unchanging.
- **Security**: Crime is nearly impossible against any established system.

## What Is Hard

- **Trust**: Requires constant, exhausting maintenance.
- **Trade**: Every exchange is a battle against checkpoint formation.
- **Innovation**: New ideas must force their way through fortified thought-patterns.
- **Love**: The most energy-intensive activity possible.
- **Death**: Bodies don't decay—they fossilize into defensive monuments. Graveyards are forests of human-shaped fortresses.
- **Large-scale cooperation**: Civilizations require staggering energy just to remain civilizations rather than fragmenting into armed hermitages.

---

## The Great Question

In this world, the central philosophical tension is: **Is openness worth the cost?** Some argue that accepting remilitarization—living in fortified solitude—is the natural and correct way. Others insist that the struggle to maintain connection is what makes existence meaningful. The universe itself seems to have an opinion: it wants everything armed, alone, and safe. Whether to defy that or accept it defines every life.
